## **Introduction**

Welcome. If you're here, you‚Äôve probably realized that being a graduate student is weird. You're a student, but also staff? You're a mentee, but you're running the lab. And to postdocs, good luck explaining to your new neighbors that you're not a professor or a student but kind of in-between.

Most of the time, this system works fine. But sometimes‚Äîusually when a paper is due or a grant is ending‚Äîthings get messy. "Collaboration" can turn into "Ghost Authorship." This guide isn't about being paranoid; it's about being **professional**. In the real world, people have contracts. In academia, we have vague verbal agreements made in hallways. This site is a collection of tools to help you build your own safety net, timestamp your brilliant ideas, and make sure that when you graduate, your CV actually reflects the work you did.

We‚Äôre going to use some fancy Open Science tools (GitHub, OSF, Zenodo), not because we love bureaucracy, but because they create **unbreakable receipts**.
